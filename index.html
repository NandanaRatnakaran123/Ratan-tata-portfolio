Career

"Tata Steel, founded in 1907, is one of the flagship companies of the Tata Group. It holds a special place in my heart, as it was established by my visionary ancestor, Jamsetji Tata. Over the years, we have become a global leader in steel manufacturing, driven by our commitment to quality, innovation, and sustainability. Our steel is used in various industries, from automotive to infrastructure, and we take immense pride in contributing to nation-building and global progress through our work. Tata Steel's values of integrity, respect, and excellence remain the pillars that guide our every endeavor.".

"As the former Chairman of Tata Sons, it was an honor to lead one of the most respected and diverse conglomerates in the world. My journey at Tata was about upholding the values set by our founder, Jamsetji Tata – integrity, commitment to excellence, and making a positive impact on society. During my tenure, I focused on expanding the Tata Group’s global presence while maintaining the core principles of ethical business practices and responsibility. Tata Sons is more than a corporate entity; it is a force for good, driven by a purpose to make a difference in the world."

Achievements

Transformed Tata Group – Took over as chairman in 1991 and made the Tata Group a globally recognized brand.

Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) Public Listing – Led TCS to its IPO in 2004, making it India's largest IT services company.

Launch of Tata Nano (2008) – Introduced the world’s cheapest car to make vehicles affordable for all.

Acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) (2008) – Acquired JLR for $2.3 billion, turning it into a profitable business.

Acquisition of Corus Steel (2007) – Tata Steel acquired Corus (UK-based) for $12 billion, making it a global player.

Tata Motors' Expansion – Led Tata Motors to become India’s largest automobile company.

Air India Revival (2022) – Tata Group reacquired Air India, bringing the airline back under Indian ownership
